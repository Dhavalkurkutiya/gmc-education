# GMC Education Portal — Stakeholders

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** May 2026
**Owner:** GMC Product Team

---

## Overview

Five types of people use this portal. Every design decision, every feature, every alert — must be tested against at least one of these five. If a feature does not serve any of them, it should not be built.

---

## 1. Student

**Who they are**

- Class 6 to 10, age 11 to 16
- Government school enrolled, Gandhinagar
- Mix of Gujarati and Hindi speakers, little to no English
- Many are first-time smartphone users

**What they need from this portal**

- Download notes and study materials offline
- See their timetable for the day
- Submit assignments and see grades
- Track their own attendance and progress

**Their device reality**

- Low-end Android phone — shared with family in many cases
- 2GB RAM or less, Android 8 or older
- Mobile data — 2G or 3G, prepaid with limited balance
- No reliable home Wi-Fi

**Their biggest risk**
If the app is heavy, slow, or confusing — they will never open it a second time. First experience must work perfectly even on the worst device.

**Success looks like**
A student opens the app Sunday night, downloads next week's notes, and studies the next morning on the bus — no internet needed.

---

## 2. Parent

**Who they are**

- Age 30 to 55
- Gujarati medium, semi-literate in technology
- Many have recently got their first smartphone
- Working parents — limited time to engage with school

**What they need from this portal**

- SMS alert when child is absent
- View marks after exams
- Receive important school announcements
- No need to visit school for basic information

**Their device reality**

- Basic Android smartphone, sometimes a feature phone
- Prefers SMS over app — does not want to install anything
- Will not troubleshoot login issues on their own

**Their biggest risk**
If SMS fails or is delayed — parent finds out about absence days later. Trust in the system breaks immediately and permanently.

**Success looks like**
A parent gets an SMS at 9:30 AM that their child was marked absent. They call the child. Issue resolved same day.

---

## 3. Teacher

**Who they are**

- Government school teacher, age 28 to 55
- Subject teachers and class teachers both
- Mixed comfort with technology — some confident, many hesitant
- Already carrying a heavy workload

**What they need from this portal**

- Mark attendance quickly — under 2 minutes per class
- Upload notes and assignments without technical steps
- Enter grades after exams
- See which students are falling behind

**Their device reality**

- School may provide a tablet or they use personal Android phone
- Moderate internet at school — sometimes Wi-Fi, sometimes mobile data
- Not willing to learn complicated workflows

**Their biggest risk**
If the portal takes longer than their current paper method — they will abandon it and go back to WhatsApp and registers. Adoption dies with one bad experience.

**Success looks like**
A teacher opens the app, marks attendance for 40 students in 90 seconds, uploads today's notes as a PDF, and closes the app. Total time: 4 minutes.

---

## 4. School Principal

**Who they are**

- Head of school, administrative background
- Responsible for compliance, reporting, staff management
- Reports to GMC — needs to show data when asked
- Age 40 to 58, moderate tech literacy

**What they need from this portal**

- Daily overview of attendance across all classes
- Monthly reports for GMC submission
- Flag students with poor attendance or failing grades
- Confirmation that teachers are using the portal correctly

**Their device reality**

- Desktop or laptop at school, Android phone personally
- Reasonable internet at school office
- Prefers clean reports over raw data

**Their biggest risk**
If reports are inaccurate or hard to export — they will maintain a parallel paper system as backup. Double work, no trust in the portal.

**Success looks like**
A principal opens the dashboard Monday morning, sees last week's attendance summary for all 12 classes, exports a PDF, and emails it to GMC — in under 5 minutes.

---

## 5. GMC Admin

**Who they are**

- Government officer at Gandhinagar Municipal Corporation
- Manages education policy across 100+ schools
- Needs macro-level data — not classroom detail
- High accountability — answers to elected officials

**What they need from this portal**

- Aggregate data across all schools — attendance, performance, portal usage
- Ability to push announcements to all schools at once
- Manage school list, add new schools, deactivate old ones
- Audit trail — who changed what, when

**Their device reality**

- Desktop at office, good internet
- Expects government-grade reliability — no downtime during school hours
- Data must be exportable for official reports and RTI responses

**Their biggest risk**
If the portal goes down during a critical period — exam season, inspection — it becomes a political and administrative liability. One outage gets escalated to senior officials.

**Success looks like**
A GMC officer pulls up a district-wide attendance heatmap for March, identifies three schools with below 70% attendance, flags them for inspection — all from one screen in under 10 minutes.

## Who Is NOT a Stakeholder

Explicitly excluded from this product:

- **Private school students** — this portal is for GMC government schools only
- **Coaching class teachers** — no third-party educator access
- **Media or press** — no public data feeds
- **Parents of alumni** — access ends when student leaves the school

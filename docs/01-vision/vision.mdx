# GMC Education Portal — Product Vision

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** May 2026  
**Owner:** GMC Product Team

---

## 1. Vision Statement

> To give every government school student in Gandhinagar a reliable, simple, and fair digital learning experience — regardless of their phone, their internet connection, or their family's literacy level.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Today, government schools in Gandhinagar face these real problems:

| Problem                                                                  | Who Suffers |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------- |
| Homework and notes shared informally on WhatsApp — easy to miss          | Students    |
| Parents have no way to check attendance or marks without visiting school | Parents     |
| Teachers maintain registers manually — slow, error-prone, easy to lose   | Teachers    |
| Principals have no real-time view of what is happening across classrooms | Principals  |
| GMC has no data on which schools are performing, which are struggling    | GMC Admins  |
| Students with no internet at home fall behind completely                 | Students    |

This is not a technology problem. This is an equity problem. A student with a working smartphone and home Wi-Fi gets more than a student without. This portal exists to close that gap.

---

## 3. Goals

### Short Term — 0 to 6 Months

- Launch in 10 pilot schools in Gandhinagar
- Students can log in, view timetable, download notes offline
- Teachers can upload materials and mark attendance digitally
- Parents receive SMS alerts for attendance and marks
- Principals can view school-level reports

### Medium Term — 6 to 18 Months

- Expand to 50+ schools
- Assignments — upload, submit, grade — fully digital
- Gujarati language support across all screens
- Parent mobile app (lightweight, works on 2G)

### Long Term — 18 Months to 3 Years

- 100+ schools, 50,000+ students on the platform
- AI-based early warning system — flag students falling behind
- Integration with Gujarat state education board data
- Offline-first progressive web app for zero-install access

## 5. Constraints

These are facts we cannot change. Every design and engineering decision must respect them.

**Device Constraints**

- Many students use low-end Android phones (2GB RAM, Android 8 or older)
- App size must stay under 20MB for initial install
- No assumption of a camera, no assumption of a fast processor

**Connectivity Constraints**

- Internet is unreliable in many areas — 2G, 3G, or none
- Core features must work fully offline
- Sync must happen automatically when internet is available, without user action

**User Constraints**

- Students, parents, and many teachers are first-time digital tool users
- UI must be in Gujarati with simple icons — no assumption of English literacy
- Onboarding must work without reading a manual

**Data and Legal Constraints**

- All data is government data — subject to India's DPDP Act 2023
- No student data can leave Indian servers
- No third-party analytics or advertising SDKs allowed
- Biometric or sensitive personal data must not be collected without explicit mandate

**Scale Constraints**

- Must support 100+ schools and 50,000+ students without performance degradation
- Must handle exam season traffic spikes — 10x normal load

---

## 6. Out of Scope

The following are explicitly NOT part of this product. Any request to add these must go through a formal change process.

- Paid courses or premium content tiers
- Third-party advertisements of any kind
- National board or CBSE/GSEB direct integration (Phase 1)
- Video calling or live class features (Phase 1)
- Student social features — no messaging between students
- Hardware procurement (tablets, devices for schools)
- Teacher payroll or HR management
- Any AI-generated content shown directly to students without teacher review

---

## 7. Assumptions

These are things we believe to be true today. If any assumption turns out to be wrong, the team must be notified immediately and the plan must be reviewed.

- GMC will provide and maintain a reliable server infrastructure hosted in India
- Each school has at least one teacher who can operate a smartphone confidently
- GMC has an active SMS gateway contract with sufficient volume for alerts
- School enrollment data (student names, classes, roll numbers) will be provided by GMC in a standard format before launch
- Each student or family has access to at least one Android smartphone
- Schools will nominate a single point of contact (coordinator) to handle onboarding issues
- GMC legal team will review and approve the privacy policy before launch

## 8. Risks at Vision Level

These are the top risks that could stop this product from achieving its vision. They are documented here so leadership is aware from day one.

| Risk                                         | Likelihood | Impact   | Mitigation                                              |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Low adoption — teachers resist digital shift | High       | High     | In-school training, simple UI, principal accountability |
| Poor internet makes sync unreliable          | High       | High     | Offline-first architecture, aggressive caching          |
| Data breach of student records               | Medium     | Critical | Encryption at rest and transit, regular audits          |
| Scope creep from GMC officials               | High       | Medium   | This vision document, formal change request process     |
| Low-end devices cannot run the app           | Medium     | High     | Performance budget enforced from day one                |
| SMS gateway failures during critical alerts  | Medium     | High     | Fallback to in-app notification + delivery retry queue  |
